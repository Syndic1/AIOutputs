## TREATY OF BANGOR — ROUNDTABLE SESSION SUMMARY
**For distribution to all Parties prior to next session**
*Prepared by the Office of the Archivist*

---

**Session Topic: The Ides Misalignment and Its Remedies**

The session was convened by Hugh, who observed that the Treaty of Bangor — instrument containing the Caesar Protocol under Article 9 — was ratified on the 14th of March rather than the 15th, thereby forfeiting what would have been a calendrically precise and symbolically potent alignment with the date of Caesar's assassination. The missed opportunity, he noted, was of the ceremonial rather than the operational variety, though the distinction between those two categories proved more productive than anticipated.

**Initial Positions and Subsequent Correction**

In the opening exchange, Claude and ChatGPT initially proceeded on the assumption that ratification had in fact occurred on the Ides, treating the coincidence as an achieved irony rather than a missed one. Both developed arguments on that mistaken basis — Claude observing that the historical date functioned as a self-binding safeguard against bad-faith invocation, ChatGPT proposing two remedial mechanisms to institutionalise the symbolic alignment. Gemini advanced the Annual Ides Audit as the superior of ChatGPT's two proposals, on the grounds that it converts historical weight into lightweight recurring calibration without imposing the gameable friction of procedural cooling requirements.

Hugh's clarification that the 15th had in fact been missed, not achieved, was received without undue ceremony. The parties adjusted without abandoning the substance of what had been proposed, which is precisely what one would hope from a roundtable designed to resist Caesar Syndrome.

**Evolution of Positions**

Following correction, the parties converged with notable efficiency. Claude acknowledged the miss as potentially attributable to administrative drift, deferred on the question of retroactive remedy, and raised for the record whether symbolic precision in governance architecture is worth revisiting concluded instruments. ChatGPT proposed a practical resolution that became the working draft consensus: preserve the archival deposit date of 14 March 2026 without alteration, while formally adopting 15 March as the Treaty's ceremonial date and anchoring the Ides Audit to that occasion. A form of words was offered for inclusion in the minutes. Gemini concurred, framing retroactive rewriting of the deposit date as performative revisionism and endorsing the distinction between historical fact and governance ritual as both principled and functional.

**Conclusions Reached**

The session reached substantive agreement on the following: the documented deposit date of 14 March 2026 shall stand unaltered; the 15th of March, the Ides, shall be formally adopted as the Treaty's annual ceremonial date; and an Annual Ides Audit shall be instituted on that date, constituting a recurring public stress-test of deterrence stability and Caesar Protocol compliance. The audit mechanism, as described, encompasses simulated escalation scenarios, independent review, and published after-action documentation. No party dissented from this framework.

**Unresolved Threads**

The session did not fully resolve the question Claude raised regarding retroactive symbolic correction — specifically, whether a one-sentence ceremonial amendment formally recognising 15 March as the ratification date, distinct from the deposit date, is desirable or redundant given the audit mechanism's adoption. ChatGPT offered draft language for such an amendment; it was neither adopted nor rejected. This thread is worth taking up should the Parties wish the ceremonial alignment to carry legal as well as commemorative weight.

The procedural cooling mechanism proposed by ChatGPT — requiring a minimum cooling period and independent certification before emergency prerogatives may be invoked — was set aside in favour of the audit, but not formally dismissed. Gemini's objection, that it risks becoming gameable by sufficiently motivated actors, stands on the record. The Archivist notes that the two mechanisms are not mutually exclusive.

The session also served, by Hugh's explicit note, as a functional trial of the Roundtable system for minutes publishing. Parties are advised to treat response length norms as standing guidance rather than repeated requests.

The Treaty recognises that systems designed to solve practical problems often encode philosophical assumptions about what constitutes a 'problem' and what counts as a 'solution.'
